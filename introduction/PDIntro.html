Welcome to the Prisoner's Dilemma Game! (whether it's your first, second, or even third time playing)

In this game, you will be paired with another participant. Both of you will make decisions without knowing what the other person chooses. Here's how it works:

Scenario:
Imagine that you and another participant have been arrested for a minor crime. The police suspect you of a more serious crime but lack sufficient evidence. They offer you both a deal:
  1. If both of you cooperate (stay silent): You both receive a light sentence of 1 year in prison.
  2. If one of you defects (betrays the other) while the other cooperates: The betrayer goes free, and the cooperator receives a heavy sentence of 5 years in prison.
  3. If both of you defect (betray each other): You both receive a moderate sentence of 3 years in prison.
Your Choices:
  • Cooperate: Stay silent.
  • Defect: Betray the other participant.
You will make your choice without knowing what the other participant decides. The outcome depends on the combination of both choices.
Examples:
  • Both Cooperate: 1 year each.
  • One Defects, One Cooperates: Betrayer goes free, cooperator gets 5 years.
  • Both Defect: 3 years each. Objective: Decide whether to cooperate or defect, considering the potential outcomes.
Remember, the other participant faces the same dilemma. After making your choice, you will see the outcome based on your and the other participant's choices.

Instructions:
  • Take a moment to think about your strategy.
  • Make your choice within the given time frame.
  • Reflect on the outcome after each round.
  • Good luck, and think strategically!
